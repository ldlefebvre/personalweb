EXAD is a native iOS marketplace I founded for exotic, adrenaline-fueled
adventures and extreme sports — the project where I went deep on building and
shipping a real, two-sided product on the App Store, end to end.

## The problem

Booking genuinely extreme experiences — think skydiving, supercar track days,
heli-adventures — is fragmented and trust-poor. EXAD set out to be a single,
curated marketplace where adventurers discover vetted experiences and providers
manage listings and bookings.

## What I built

As founder, I built the product end to end: a native iOS client in Swift and
SwiftUI — discovery and search, rich experience-detail screens, the booking flow,
real-time chat, maps, a custom camera, deep links, push notifications, and cached
images — on a Firebase backend. I wired up Stripe Connect payments through a
Node.js layer, automated transactional emails, and designed the branding with
custom SF Symbols and Figma/Photoshop.

## What I took away

EXAD taught me how a production iOS app actually ships — App Store review,
release management, payment integration, and the discipline of building a polished
two-sided marketplace where both buyers and providers depend on the same app.
